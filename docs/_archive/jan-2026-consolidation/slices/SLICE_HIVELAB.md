## The Upstream Problem

Students have ideas. Ideas die.

"We should have a signup form." → Never happens.
"We should poll the members." → Too much effort.
"We need to track who's coming." → Someone will do it manually.
"We should collect feedback." → Maybe next semester.

**The gap between "we should" and "it exists" is where student initiative goes to die.**

Why does the gap exist?

1. **Friction** — Building anything takes time, skills, or money they don't have
2. **Fragmentation** — They'd have to use 5 different external services
3. **Impermanence** — Whatever they build doesn't connect to anything else
4. **Invisibility** — No one sees it, no one cares, it disappears

**HiveLab closes the gap.**

"We should" → "It exists" in 60 seconds.

## Standalone vs Contextual

### Standalone
Exists on its own. Share link. Anyone can use it.

- Share anywhere (GroupMe, Instagram, text)
- No HIVE account required to respond
- Lives outside the platform

**Good for:** Quick utilities, sharing externally, testing.

### In a Space
Added to a space. Part of the community.

- Audience built in
- Context provided ("our club's signup")
- Persists with the space

**Good for:** Org utilities, community resources.

### As an Event
Temporal properties. Date, time, RSVP, countdown.

- Shows in calendars and feeds
- Anticipation mechanics
- Post-event memory capture

**Good for:** Anything with a when.
