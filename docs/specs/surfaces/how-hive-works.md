# How HIVE Works: The Complete System

**HIVE is what happens when you give a campus an AI that creates, a feed that distributes, and spaces that respond.**

This document describes how the entire product operates as one system — not four screens bolted together, but a single organism where every action feeds the next.

## The System: Four Surfaces, One Loop

HIVE has four surfaces. Each does one job. Together, they form a loop that accelerates with every action.

```
┌─────────┐     ┌─────────┐     ┌─────────┐     ┌─────────┐
│  BUILD  │────→│ SPACES  │────→│  FEED   │────→│ PROFILE │
│         │     │         │     │         │     │         │
│ Create  │     │ Land +  │     │ Reach + │     │ Proof + │
│         │     │ Engage  │     │ Discover│     │ Motivate│
└────┬────┘     └─────────┘     └─────────┘     └────┬────┘
     │                                                │
     └────────────── "It worked. Again." ─────────────┘
```

### Build: "Say something."
The creation engine. A student types a sentence. HIVE classifies the intent, matches it to a format (poll, bracket, RSVP, ranker, quiz, or custom), and generates a working interactive experience. Three tiers of capability:

- **Shell match** (~500ms): Intent matches a known format. Instant, reliable, polished.
- **Code generation** (~15s): HIVE writes custom code using the HIVE SDK. Anything the student can describe.
- **Open canvas** (future): Full creative freedom for novel experiences.

Build doesn't ask "where should this go?" It creates first, distributes automatically.

### Spaces: "Your campus responds."
The living containers where creations meet their audience. 650+ spaces pre-seeded from real UB organizations. Five types:

- **Student Org** — clubs and organizations
- **University** — official university entities
- **Residential** — dorms and housing
- **Greek Life** — fraternities and sororities
- **HIVE Exclusive** — anything a student creates on HIVE

**Spaces work without leaders.** HIVE generates contextually relevant creations for every pre-seeded space. A student joins "UB Chess Club" and finds a poll already running — "Best chess opening?" with 34 votes. No human created it. HIVE did. When a leader eventually claims the space, they inherit momentum, not emptiness.

**Anyone can create in a space.** Slash commands (`/poll`, `/bracket`, `/rsvp`) are available to all members, not just leaders. The creation bottleneck disappears.

### Feed: "What's alive right now?"
The front page of campus. The feed is its own surface — a student can get full value from the feed without ever joining a space. They vote on polls, RSVP to events, discover what's happening.

Events and creations are peers. Events drive real-world action ("go somewhere tonight"). Creations drive digital engagement ("vote on this right now"). Both are interactive. Both are first-class.

**Inline engagement.** You vote on a poll IN the feed card. You RSVP to an event IN the feed card. No navigation required. The feed is a participation surface, not a reading surface.

### Profile: "It worked. Again."
The creator's receipt. A student who built a poll sees "47 people participated in your apps" on their profile. Each creation shows its own number — "47 votes", "12 RSVPs". The profile converts effort into evidence and evidence into motivation.

**Only human creations appear on profiles.** AI-generated content in spaces is not attributed to anyone. The profile is proof of human effort.

For non-creators, the profile is campus identity — who you are, where you belong, what you care about. But its strategic job is closing the creation loop.

## What Makes This Feel Like the Future

### 1. The AI Creates Alongside You

Most platforms wait for humans to produce content. HIVE doesn't wait. It reads the metadata of 650+ real campus organizations and generates contextually relevant creations — polls, brackets, rankers — that feel native to each space.

This means:
- The campus is never empty. Day one, every space has something to interact with.
- The feed is never stale. AI creations rotate, keeping the front page fresh.
- New members always have something to do. No "join and wait for someone to post."

The AI steps back as humans step up. When real students start creating, their creations take priority. The AI was the spark. Humans are the fire.

### 2. Creation is One Sentence

The gap between "I have an idea" and "people are using it" collapsed from days to seconds. On every other platform, reaching your campus with something interactive requires building a form, designing a page, setting up distribution, and hoping people click. On HIVE, you type one sentence.

This isn't a feature. It's a fundamental shift in who gets to be a creator. The bar drops from "people who know how to build things" to "people who can type a sentence." A freshman with zero followers can reach 200 people in 2 hours.

### 3. The Campus is Interactive, Not Static

Before HIVE, campus is bulletin boards, email lists, and GroupMe messages. Static. One-directional. You can't ask your campus a question and get an answer.

With HIVE, you can. "Best dining hall?" — your campus answers. "Who's coming Saturday?" — your campus answers. "Rate the bathrooms in this building" — your campus answers. The campus becomes a living, responsive system.

### 4. Distribution is Automatic

On Instagram, you need followers. On GroupMe, you need to be in the right chat. On email, you need a list. On HIVE, you need nothing. Create something → it enters the campus feed automatically → people engage.

Every creation gets:
- A standalone URL (shareable anywhere, works without an account)
- Placement in the campus feed (discovered by anyone on campus)
- Placement in its source space (seen by space members)
- Push notifications to space members (pulled back into the app)

No distribution decisions required. The creator focuses on the idea. HIVE handles reach.

### 5. Participation Happens Before Commitment

On every other campus platform, participating means showing up: attending a meeting, joining a GroupMe, introducing yourself. The social cost is high, especially for freshmen, transfer students, and commuters.

On HIVE, you vote on a poll. You RSVP to an event. You rank something. You're participating in your campus before you ever walk into a room. When you do show up, you're not a stranger — you're the person who voted, who RSVP'd, who ranked. You belong through action, not introduction.

### 6. Spaces Get Smarter Over Time

Every time someone creates a poll, bracket, or RSVP in a space, that space gains a new capability. A space isn't a static page — it's an evolving organism that gets more useful with every creation.

GroupMe never gets smarter. It's the same text box forever. A HIVE space accumulates interactive experiences over time. The more people create, the more valuable the space becomes.

## The Three Loops

HIVE runs on three interlocking loops. Each one accelerates the others.

### Loop 1: The Creation Loop (Individual)

```
Create → Place → See engagement → Create again
```

One person's cycle. They build something, it reaches people, they see the numbers, they build another. This is the retention mechanism for creators. The profile is what closes this loop — it shows proof that creating works.

**Fuel:** Every notification ("Alex voted on your poll"), every profile stat ("47 people participated"), every live counter (watching votes tick up in real-time).

**Stall point:** If the creator sees "0 votes" for 24 hours, the loop dies. Engagement velocity matters more than total engagement. 5 votes in 1 hour is better than 50 votes over a month.

### Loop 2: The Distribution Loop (Social)

```
Creation appears in feed → Student engages → Discovers source space → Joins → Sees more creations → Engages more
```

How creations spread and spaces grow. The feed distributes creations to the campus. Students engage. They discover the space the creation came from. They join. Now they see more creations from that space. They engage with those too.

**Fuel:** The feed's inline engagement (vote without leaving), the post-engagement join prompt ("Join UB Dining?"), the space's creation density (more creations = more reasons to stay).

**Stall point:** If the feed has no creations worth engaging with. AI creations solve the cold start, but they must be good enough to earn a vote.

### Loop 3: The Inspiration Loop (Cultural)

```
Student sees creation with high engagement → "I could do that" → Creates their own → Their creation inspires someone else
```

How consumers become creators. A student sees "47 votes" on someone's poll and thinks "I could make one about..." This is the TikTok pattern — watching content inspires creating content.

**Fuel:** Visible engagement numbers on every creation. Creator names on feed cards (tappable to profile). The profile showing that one simple poll can reach 47 people. The bar feels low enough to clear.

**Stall point:** If creator profiles look intimidating (too many apps, complex stats). The profile must make creation feel achievable, not exclusive. One app with 23 votes should look like success.

## How the Surfaces Connect

### Every Surface Links to Every Other

No dead ends. Every surface has outbound connections to all three others.

| From | To Build | To Spaces | To Feed | To Profile |
|------|----------|-----------|---------|------------|
| **Build** | — | Post-creation placement | — | "View your apps" |
| **Spaces** | Slash commands, SparkleCreateSheet, FAB | — | — | Member names → profile |
| **Feed** | "Make your own" CTA | Space names → space page, Join button | — | Creator names → profile |
| **Profile** | "Build your first app" CTA | Space cards → space page | — | — |

### Context Travels Between Surfaces

When you move between surfaces, you don't start from scratch.

- **Space → Build:** `?spaceId` travels. Build shows "Making for UB Engineering." After creation, auto-places in that space.
- **Feed → Space:** Tapping a creation's space name lands you in the space with context about what you just engaged with.
- **Build → Space:** After placing a creation, you land in the space's Apps tab, seeing your creation live.
- **Feed → Profile:** Tapping a creator's name shows their profile with shared context (mutual spaces, shared interests).

### Notifications Pull You Back

When you're not in the app, notifications are the connective tissue.

| What happened | Who gets notified | Where they land |
|---|---|---|
| New creation placed in your space | All space members | Space page |
| Someone used your creation | Creator | The creation |
| Your creation hit 50 votes | Creator | The creation |
| New event in your space | All space members | Space events tab |
| Event starting in 30 min | RSVP'd attendees | Event detail |
| Someone joined your space | Space leader | Space page |

Notifications are rate-limited to prevent fatigue. Creator notifications batch ("3 people used your poll" instead of 3 separate notifications). Event reminders fire once. Member joins cap at 5/day per space.

## The Standalone URL: HIVE's Viral Mechanism

Every creation has a URL at `/t/[toolId]` that works without an account. This is how HIVE escapes the app and reaches non-users.

```
Student creates poll → Gets standalone URL → Shares in GroupMe/iMessage/Instagram
                                                    ↓
                                          Non-user opens link
                                                    ↓
                                          Sees poll, votes (no login)
                                                    ↓
                                          Sees "Made with HIVE" footer
                                                    ↓
                                          Thinks "I could make one of these"
                                                    ↓
                                          Downloads HIVE → Creates own
```

The standalone URL is the acquisition engine. The feed and spaces are the retention engine. Together, they form the growth flywheel.

## What HIVE Is Not

- **Not a social network.** No feed of posts, no likes, no follower counts as the product. Creations are the content.
- **Not a messaging app.** Chat exists inside spaces, but HIVE isn't competing with GroupMe for messaging. It's competing for what happens after the message.
- **Not a form builder.** The AI figures out the format. You never see a form builder interface.
- **Not a content platform.** You don't scroll and consume. You scroll and participate.
- **Not an admin tool.** Institutional use is a later wedge. Launch is student-to-student.
