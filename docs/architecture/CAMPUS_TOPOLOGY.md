## Actor Analysis

### Primary Actors

#### 1. Students (Members)

**Freshmen (~5,500/year)**
- Highest discovery need
- Forming campus identity
- Most receptive to new platforms
- Fear of missing out (FOMO) driven
- **Wedge:** "Discover your people"

**Sophomores-Juniors (~11,000)**
- Established but seeking depth
- Leadership aspirations emerging
- Time-constrained (academics + activities)
- **Wedge:** "Stay connected to what matters"

**Seniors (~5,500)**
- Disengaging from campus
- Career-focused
- Alumni transition
- **Wedge:** "Leave a legacy"

#### 2. Student Leaders (~2,000 active)

**Executive Board Members**
- Heavy administrative burden
- Manage 5+ platforms
- Care about engagement metrics
- Need member retention tools
- **Wedge:** "Run your org from one place"

**Emerging Leaders**
- Want to start new initiatives
- Need visibility and legitimacy
- Limited resources
- **Wedge:** "Build your community"

#### 3. Faculty/Staff (~6,000)

**Student Affairs Staff**
- Responsible for student engagement
- Need oversight and metrics
- Budget holders
- **Wedge:** "Visibility into student life"

**Faculty Advisors**
- Legally required for student orgs
- Minimal engagement typically
- **Wedge:** "Effortless oversight"

### Actor Pain Points Matrix

| Actor | Current Pain | Severity | HIVE Solution |
|-------|--------------|----------|---------------|
| New Student | Don't know what exists | High | Discovery feed |
| Active Member | Miss events, updates | Medium | Personalized notifications |
| Club Leader | Platform fragmentation | Very High | Single hub |
| SA Staff | No engagement visibility | High | Admin analytics |
| Faculty Advisor | Out of loop | Low | Digest notifications |

## Wedge Strategy

### What is a Wedge?

A wedge is the initial use case that gets users onto the platform. It must be:
1. **Painful enough** that users will try something new
2. **Narrow enough** that HIVE can be best-in-class
3. **Expandable** into broader platform usage

### Primary Wedge: Space Discovery

#### The Problem

New students (5,500 freshmen + transfers annually) arrive on campus knowing nothing about the 300+ student organizations. Current solutions fail:

- **Campus Labs/UB Linked:** Static directory, no social proof, feels official/boring
- **Club Fair:** Once per semester, overwhelming, forget everything
- **Instagram:** Must already follow, no discovery mechanism
- **Word of mouth:** Depends on social luck

#### Why This Is a Wedge

1. **Timing:** Concentrated need (August-September)
2. **Frequency:** One-time conversion, then retained
3. **Emotion:** FOMO, belonging, identity formation
4. **Outcome:** Membership in spaces (network effect)

#### HIVE Solution

```
Discovery Experience:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Welcome to HIVE                         │
│                                         │
│ Based on your interests:                │
│                                         │
│ [Jazz Club]          48 members         │
│  "Open jam tonight"  ████████ Active    │
│                                         │
│ [Startup Club]       124 members        │
│  "Pitch competition" ██████████ Hot     │
│                                         │
```

#### Conversion Funnel

```

Week 1-2: Campus marketing, orientation

Ongoing: Feed consumption, event attendance
```

## External Factors

### Tailwinds (Working in Our Favor)

| Factor | Impact | How to Leverage |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| **Post-COVID social deficit** | High desire for connection | Emphasize community, belonging |
| **Platform fatigue** | Students tired of managing many apps | Consolidation value prop |
| **AI advancement** | New features possible | AI-powered discovery, content |
| **Gen Z platform fluency** | Quick adoption of new tools | Mobile-first, modern UX |
| **Creator economy awareness** | Students understand "building audience" | Leader tools, analytics |

### Headwinds (Working Against Us)

| Factor | Risk | Mitigation |
|--------|------|------------|
| **Network effects chicken-egg** | Empty platform isn't useful | Seed with leader content, launch with critical mass |
| **Platform switching cost** | Students already on Instagram/GroupMe | Don't replace, augment; bridge to existing |
| **Short attention spans** | Hard to establish new habit | Notifications, FOMO mechanics |
| **Academic priority** | Students are busy | Must be low-friction, valuable quickly |
| **University IT resistance** | May see as competitor | Position as complement, not replacement |

### Seasonal Factors

| Period | Opportunity | Risk |
|--------|-------------|------|
| **August-September** | Orientation, club fair, peak discovery | Competition for attention |
| **October** | Homecoming, settled into routines | Midterm stress |
| **November** | Pre-finals, projects due | Low engagement period |
| **January** | Fresh start energy, new semester | Short window |
| **February-March** | Spring activities ramp up | Weather dependent (Buffalo) |
| **April** | End-of-year events | Graduation focus |
| **Summer** | Low campus population | Build and test |

### Regulatory/Institutional Factors

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|--------------|
| **FERPA** | Student privacy protection; can't access university data |
| **Student org registration** | Must be registered with SA for official status |
| **University branding** | Can't misrepresent as official UB product |
| **Data privacy (GDPR-like)** | Must protect student data |
| **Accessibility (ADA)** | Platform must be accessible |

## Go-to-Market Sequence

### Phase 0: Pre-Launch (Now - Launch)

**Objective:** Build critical mass before public launch

1. **Leader Recruitment (20-30 key clubs)**
   - Target: Largest, most active organizations
   - Value prop: Early access, shape the platform
   - Commitment: Create space, post content

2. **Content Seeding**
   - Import existing events from UB Linked
   - Leaders pre-populate their spaces
   - Ensure feed isn't empty on day 1

3. **Ambassador Program**
   - 10-20 student ambassadors
   - Paid or credited
   - Word of mouth in their circles

### Phase 1: Soft Launch (Week 1-2)

**Objective:** Controlled growth with feedback loop

1. **Invite-only access**
   - Leaders invite their members
   - Cap total users (500-1000)
   - Iterate rapidly on feedback

2. **Focus metrics:**
   - Onboarding completion rate
   - Day 1, Day 7 retention
   - Spaces joined per user
   - Critical bugs

### Phase 2: Public Launch (Week 3-4)

**Objective:** Campus-wide awareness

1. **Launch Event**
   - Physical presence at Student Union
   - Social media campaign
   - Coordinate with SA if possible

2. **Virality Mechanics**
   - "Invite friends to your space"
   - Social proof (X friends are on HIVE)
   - FOMO notifications

### Phase 3: Density Building (Month 2-3)

**Objective:** Reach critical mass on campus

1. **Target:** 30% of student body (~9,600)
2. **Focus:** Engagement depth, not just signups
3. **Metrics:**
   - Weekly active users (WAU)
   - Posts per week
   - Events with >20 RSVPs

### Phase 4: Expansion Consideration (Month 4+)

**Objective:** Prove model before expanding

1. **Success criteria for UB:**
   - 30%+ student penetration
   - 50%+ of major clubs active
   - Positive unit economics
   - NPS > 40

2. **Next campus selection:**
   - Similar size/culture
   - Geographic proximity (regional first)
   - Existing connections (founder network)

## Competitive Moats

### Moat 1: Network Density

**Description:** The value of HIVE increases with users on the same campus. A student's friends, clubs, and events are all UB-specific.

**Defense:** No national platform can match campus-specific density. GroupMe has groups but no discovery. Instagram has followers but no membership.

### Moat 2: Campus-Specific AI

**Description:** Every post, event, and interaction trains HIVE's understanding of UB specifically. "UB Late Night" means something to the model.

**Defense:** This knowledge compounds. A competitor starting fresh at UB would need to rebuild this context from scratch.

### Moat 3: Leader Investment

**Description:** Once a club leader sets up their space, configures tools, and builds member base, switching costs are high.

**Defense:** Leaders won't migrate if their members are on HIVE. And members won't leave if their clubs are on HIVE.

### Moat 4: Institutional Relationship

**Description:** If HIVE becomes the de facto student engagement platform, the university has incentive to support it.

**Defense:** University endorsement (even informal) legitimizes HIVE and creates barrier for competitors.
