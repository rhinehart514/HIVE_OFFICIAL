### Color System
- **Gold gradient** (`#FFD700` ‚Üí `#FFA500`): HIVE brand, active states, CTAs
- **Dark theme** (`#0A0A0A`): Default background, focus on content
- **White text** (100% / 70% / 50%): Primary, secondary, tertiary hierarchy

### Typography
- **Display**: 28px/32px ‚Äî Headlines, hero text
- **Title**: 22px/26px ‚Äî Section headers
- **Body**: 14px/20px ‚Äî Default text

- **Lucide icons**: Consistent stroke weight, 20-24px size
- **Custom**: HIVE logo (hexagon with gold gradient), ritual banners (gold glow effect)

- **HIVE**: Student-run, instant creation, bottom-up coordination

- **HIVE**: Feed-first, campus culture, discovery-by-default

- **HIVE**: Feed + spaces + tools, public coordination, campus ecosystem

## üí° BRAND PRINCIPLES

### 1. Autonomy, not permission
The future of student life isn't granted by policy ‚Äî it's built through initiative.

**Example**: HiveLab lets any student build a tool in 10 minutes. No approval process, no IT department, no waiting.

### 2. Ownership as identity
The same people who live the culture should shape its tools.

**Example**: Rituals are created by students, for students. The Founding 100 was picked by 2,000 student votes, not admin selection.

### 3. Design over bureaucracy
Systems should move as fast as students do.

**Example**: Join a space ‚Üí See feed ‚Üí Post event ‚Üí All in < 30 seconds. No forms, no requests, no delays.

### 4. Momentum through creation
Every post, tool, or event isn't content ‚Äî it's proof that the campus can organize itself.

**Example**: 100+ tools built in HiveLab first semester = proof students can build infrastructure faster than institutions.

### 5. No waiting
The age of waiting for "the official app" is over.

**Example**: When UB didn't have event coordination tools, students built them. When Instagram's feed wasn't campus-focused, students made HIVE.
