# HIVE Value Propositions

## North Star
50 weekly active creators within 2 weeks of UB launch.

## One-Liner
**"Say something. Your campus responds."**

It doesn't matter if it's a poll, a bathroom ranker, or something nobody's thought of. You type a sentence. HIVE makes it real. Your campus uses it.

---

## Transformational Value Props

These are not incremental improvements. These are things that were impossible before HIVE.

### 1. Zero-to-Audience in One Sentence
Before HIVE, reaching 200 people on your campus required a following, a platform, a distribution channel, and time. A nobody freshman with zero followers could not reach 200 people with a single action. Period.

HIVE makes that possible. You type a sentence. It becomes a live experience. It enters the campus feed. People engage. You went from zero to audience without earning it first.

- **What a student says:** "I made a poll 2 hours ago and 200 people voted??"
- **Measured by:** engagement per creation, % of creations with >10 interactions

### 2. Campus Becomes Responsive
Before HIVE, campus is static infrastructure. Buildings, bulletin boards, email lists. You can't ask your campus a question and get an answer.

With HIVE, the campus becomes something you talk to and get a response from. "Best dining hall?" — your campus answers. "Who's coming Saturday?" — your campus answers.

- **What a student says:** "I asked which dining hall is best and the whole campus has opinions"
- **Measured by:** unique respondents per creation, response velocity

### 3. Ideas Become Real Without Skills
The gap between "I had a thought" and "people are using it" is enormous for most students. You'd need to code something, or use a form builder, or know someone who can help.

With HIVE, the gap is one sentence and 15 seconds. No code. No form builder. No design skills.

- **What a student says:** "bro I literally just typed a sentence and it made the whole thing"
- **Measured by:** time-to-first-creation, first-try success rate

### 4. Participation Without Social Cost
Joining a community normally means showing up physically, introducing yourself, being the new person. For a lonely freshman or transfer student, the social cost is enormous.

With HIVE, you vote on a poll. You RSVP to an event. You play a quiz. You're participating in your campus before you ever walk into a room. You belong through action, not introduction.

- **What a student says:** "I found this club through a bracket and now I'm going thursday"
- **Measured by:** % of first interactions that are anonymous/low-commitment, space joins preceded by app interaction

## User-Segment Value Props

### Creators (anyone with an idea)
**"I typed one sentence and 200 strangers on my campus engaged with it."**

- Not limited to org leaders. Anyone with a thought can create.
- The creation could be a poll, a quiz, a bathroom ranker, a bingo card — anything.
- Distribution is automatic: campus feed + standalone URL + optional space placement.
- Feedback is real-time: watch the engagement counter tick up.
- Win condition: Creator checks back hours later and the number is way bigger than expected. That gap between expected and actual reach is the viral trigger.

### Org Leaders (coordination power)
**"47 votes from one sentence. No more GroupMe spam."**

- JTBD: Reach 60 members with something functional, not just a post.
- Current alternatives: GroupMe (buried), Google Forms (broken), Instagram (12 likes), email (ignored).
- HIVE: Type what you need → working experience in seconds → place in space → members notified → responses come in.
- Win condition: Leader says "this is faster and more effective than my current setup."

### Members (discovery + participation)
**"I opened HIVE and voted on the best dining hall. 200 people voted."**

- JTBD: Find what's happening, participate in one tap.
- The feed shows live creations with engagement counts. You scroll, you tap, you interact.
- You discover communities through what they create, not through a directory.
- Win condition: Student opens HIVE before texting the group chat.

### Growth (standalone virality)
**"My friend sent me a link, I voted, I saw 'Made with HIVE.'"**

- Every creation has a standalone URL that works without auth.
- Shared in GroupMe, iMessage, Instagram stories.
- Non-users can interact (vote, RSVP) without signing up.
- "Made with HIVE" footer + "Create your own" CTA.
- Win condition: 20%+ of new signups come from standalone links.

## What HIVE Is

- An AI that turns student ideas into live campus experiences
- A place where creations spread and people engage
- The only platform where a student goes from thought to audience in one sentence

## What HIVE is NOT

- Not a social network (no feed of posts, no likes, no followers-as-the-product)
- Not a messaging app (chat exists inside spaces, not as the product)
- Not a form builder (the AI figures out the format, you don't)
- Not a content platform (the creations ARE the content — interactive, live, functional)
- Not an admin tool (institutional use is a later wedge, not the launch play)

## Value Mechanisms (for feature evaluation)

Every feature should strengthen at least one of these:

| Mechanism | How it works | Measured by |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Reach** | Creation gets to people via feed + notification + standalone URL | Unique viewers per creation, notification tap rate |
| **Engagement** | People interact with creations (vote, RSVP, rate, submit) | Interactions per creation, interaction rate |
| **Speed** | Time from "I have an idea" to "it's live and people are using it" | Time-to-first-creation, time-to-first-engagement |
| **Aliveness** | Surfaces feel active even at low density | Presence indicators, "Since you left," time-based content |
| **Loop closure** | One action leads naturally to the next | % of creations with >0 engagement, repeat creation rate |
| **Surprise** | The output exceeds expectations — "wait, it can do that?" | Creation diversity, novel format usage, code gen hit rate |

## The Moments That Matter

Students don't screenshot value props. They screenshot moments. The product's job is to engineer these:

**Moment 1: The creation surprise.** They type a messy sentence. Something polished and functional comes back. "Wait... that's it?"

**Moment 2: The first response.** Someone interacts. Push notification: "Someone voted on your poll." The first ping means someone out there is using your thing.

**Moment 3: The number shock.** They check back. 47... 89... 142. Way bigger than expected. This is the screenshot moment. This is what gets texted to friends.

**Moment 4: The "I could do that."** Someone sees a friend's creation with 200 engagements. They think "I could make one about..." Now they're a creator. The cycle repeats.
